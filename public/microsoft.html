Women’s Health
Why Every Woman Should Know
Her DEXA Numbers
1 in 3

Women over 50 will experience a bone fracture due to weak bones

DEXA is the only scan that measures bone mineral density with clinical precision — catching bone loss years before a fracture.

80%

Of osteoporosis patients are women

Bone density peaks around age 30 and declines after. Early detection changes outcomes dramatically.

Hormonal Health

Menopause & Body Composition

Hormonal changes affect where and how women store fat. DEXA reveals visceral fat — the metabolically dangerous fat that increases during menopause, even without weight gain on the scale.

Visceral Fat

The Hidden Risk

Women with normal BMI can still carry high visceral fat — linked to heart disease, Type 2 diabetes, and metabolic syndrome. DEXA quantifies it. BMI cannot.

Muscle Health

Sarcopenia Starts Earlier Than You Think

Women lose muscle mass 2x faster after 50. Tracking lean mass through DEXA helps design the right exercise and nutrition plan before it’s too late.
